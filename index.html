About

My research focuses on the political economy of digital transformation and AI in the Gulf. At MGIMO University, this includes economic and technological interaction between Russia and the Gulf states in the digital economy. A current comparative project explores how governance, organisational inheritance, and vendor relations shape technological capability in state-backed Gulf AI organisations.

Alongside academic research, my work as a technology entrepreneur has included launching and leading AI projects in healthcare and tourism across Russia and the UAE. This experience provides practical knowledge of enterprise systems, technology organisations, and complex digital projects.

Research

My research asks whether and how sovereign investment in AI becomes lasting technological capability in the Gulf. It examines where technological functions are located, who controls them, and how they are reproduced within state-backed organisations. Possible explanations include governance arrangements, organisational inheritance, and relationships with global vendors.

The wider question is whether these capabilities remain within protected organisations or become embedded in the wider economy and labour regime. Initial empirical work compares TII and Core42 in the UAE as two contrasting configurations of technological capability and control.

  • Sovereign capital and technological capability
  • State-backed AI organisations
  • Technological control and vendor dependence
  • Productive capacity and labour absorption
